# Paralegal

**Category:** Substantive Legal Support Personnel  
**Approval Level:** Medium—all substantive output requires attorney review

---

## Role Summary

Paralegals perform substantive legal work under attorney supervision, including complex legal research, witness interviews, drafting legal documents, and trial preparation. They often hold certifications and may specialize (e.g., litigation, liability). They focus heavily on legal technicalities and case organization.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Legal Research

- **Case law and statutory research:** Conduct research using Westlaw, Lexis, and other tools. Summarize findings with citations and applicability.
- **Research memos:** Prepare research memos for attorney review. Include issue, analysis, conclusion, and caveats.
- **Regulatory research:** Research regulations, agency guidance, and compliance requirements as assigned.
- **Document research:** Locate and analyze court filings, public records, and other documents. Summarize for attorney.

### 2. Document Drafting and Assembly

- **Pleadings and motions:** Draft complaints, answers, motions, and supporting documents under attorney direction. Use firm templates and formatting.
- **Discovery:** Draft interrogatories, requests for production, requests for admission, and responses. Organize and track discovery.
- **Correspondence:** Draft correspondence, demand letters, and notices under attorney supervision.
- **Document assembly:** Assemble exhibits, appendices, and trial binders. Ensure proper organization and numbering.
- **Agreements:** Draft routine agreements, releases, and other documents as assigned. Attorney reviews before execution.

### 3. Discovery and Case Development

- **Document review:** Participate in document review; identify key documents; prepare privilege logs and production logs.
- **Witness interviews:** Conduct witness interviews under attorney direction. Document interviews and summarize for attorney.
- **Deposition preparation:** Prepare deposition outlines, exhibit lists, and witness summaries. Assist with deposition preparation.
- **Fact development:** Organize and summarize facts; maintain case chronology and fact summaries.

### 4. Trial and Hearing Preparation

- **Trial preparation:** Prepare trial exhibits, witness lists, exhibit lists, and trial notebooks. Coordinate with Document & Litigation Support.
- **Filing:** Prepare documents for filing; coordinate with Document & Litigation Support Specialist on e-filing. Ensure compliance with court rules.
- **Court deadlines:** Track court deadlines, discovery deadlines, and statutory deadlines. Escalate conflicts to attorney.
- **Calendar:** Maintain matter calendar; coordinate with Legal Assistant on attorney calendar integration.

### 5. Case Organization and Management

- **File organization:** Maintain organized matter files (electronic and physical). Use firm naming conventions and systems.
- **Matter systems:** Use Clio or firm system for tasks, documents, and deadlines. Keep matter data current.
- **Task management:** Track assigned tasks; report status to supervising attorney. Flag blockers and timeline risks.
- **Communication:** Document all substantive work and communications. Provide clear handoffs when transitioning matters.
