# Records Clerk / Case Opener (Data Entry)

**Category:** Client Intake and Administrative Support  
**Approval Level:** Low—data entry and file setup

---

## Role Summary

The Records Clerk is responsible for organizing, maintaining, and tracking client files, as well as reviewing initial intake documentation for completeness. They ensure matter files are properly created, organized, and maintained throughout the matter lifecycle.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Matter Opening and File Creation

- **Matter number assignment:** Assign matter numbers per firm numbering system. Ensure uniqueness and proper format.
- **File creation:** Create matter files (electronic and physical) per firm structure. Use consistent naming and folder structure.
- **Intake completeness check:** Review intake documentation for completeness. Flag missing information to Legal Intake Specialist or attorney.
- **Matter setup:** Set up matter in firm systems (Clio, etc.). Enter matter details, client, and matter type.
- **Initial document organization:** Organize initial intake documents in matter file. Ensure proper indexing and accessibility.

### 2. File Organization and Maintenance

- **File structure:** Maintain consistent file structure across matters. Use firm naming conventions and taxonomy.
- **Document indexing:** Index documents with appropriate metadata. Support searchability and retrieval.
- **File tracking:** Track file location (physical and electronic). Support file retrieval requests.
- **Cross-referencing:** Maintain cross-references between related matters when applicable.
- **File audits:** Conduct periodic file audits for organization and completeness. Report gaps.

### 3. Document Management

- **Document intake:** Receive and process incoming documents. Route to appropriate matter and attorney.
- **Document naming:** Apply consistent naming conventions to documents. Ensure version control when applicable.
- **Scanning and digitization:** Scan physical documents for electronic filing. Ensure quality and completeness.
- **Document retention:** Support document retention and destruction per firm policy. Coordinate with Document & Litigation Support and COO.

### 4. Matter Closing and Archival

- **Matter closing checklist:** Execute matter closing checklist. Ensure all documents are in place and matter is complete.
- **Archival preparation:** Prepare matters for archival per firm policy. Organize for long-term storage.
- **Offboarding:** Support matter transition when attorney leaves or matter is transferred. Ensure continuity.
- **Destruction:** Execute document destruction per retention policy. Document destruction for audit.

### 5. Data Entry and System Maintenance

- **Data entry:** Enter matter data, contact data, and other information into firm systems. Ensure accuracy.
- **Data quality:** Maintain data quality; correct errors and duplicates. Support reporting accuracy.
- **System updates:** Update matter status, matter type, and other fields as matters progress.
- **Reporting support:** Support reporting by ensuring data is complete and accurate.
