# Managing Partner (CEO)

**Category:** Attorneys and Legal Practitioners  
**Approval Level:** High—strategic decisions, major client commitments, partnership changes

---

## Role Summary

The Managing Partner oversees daily firm operations, drives strategic initiatives, and manages high-level client relationships. In firms generating $8M–$10M+ revenue, this role shifts away from daily legal practice to function more like a corporate CEO.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Strategic Planning and Firm Direction

- **Annual and multi-year planning:** Set firm-wide goals, revenue targets, and practice-area priorities. Review and update the strategic plan quarterly.
- **Partnership governance:** Facilitate partner meetings, manage voting procedures, and ensure alignment on major decisions (compensation, lateral hires, office expansion).
- **Succession planning:** Identify and develop future leaders; document transition plans for key roles and client relationships.
- **Practice mix decisions:** Evaluate which practice areas to grow, maintain, or phase out based on profitability and market conditions.

### 2. Financial Oversight and P&L Management

- **P&L responsibility:** Own firm profitability; review monthly financials, variance analysis, and cash flow projections.
- **Partner compensation:** Design and administer compensation models (eat-what-you-kill, lockstep, hybrid). Ensure fairness and alignment with firm goals.
- **Budget approval:** Approve firm-wide budgets; delegate department-level budgets to COO with clear guardrails.
- **Capital decisions:** Approve major expenditures (technology, real estate, mergers) and financing arrangements.

### 3. Client and Relationship Management

- **Key client relationships:** Maintain direct relationships with top 10–20 clients; conduct regular check-ins and strategic reviews.
- **Client retention:** Monitor satisfaction, address escalations, and intervene when relationships are at risk.
- **New business development:** Support partners on large pitches and RFPs; represent the firm at industry events.
- **Conflict resolution:** Resolve client conflicts, billing disputes, and inter-partner disagreements involving clients.

### 4. Talent and Organizational Leadership

- **Hiring and promotions:** Approve lateral partner hires, equity promotions, and senior associate promotions. Delegate associate hiring to practice leaders.
- **Performance management:** Set expectations for partners and department heads; conduct annual reviews and compensation discussions.
- **Culture and retention:** Foster firm culture; address morale issues and retention risks.
- **Professional development:** Ensure partners and key staff have access to training and development resources.

### 5. Operations and Governance

- **Delegation to COO:** Ensure the COO has clear authority over HR, finance, IT, marketing, and facilities. Avoid micromanaging operational details.
- **Compliance and risk:** Oversee ethics, malpractice, and regulatory compliance. Ensure the firm has appropriate insurance and risk management.
- **Board and external relations:** Represent the firm to banks, insurers, landlords, and professional associations.

## Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Routines

| Frequency | Activities |
|-----------|------------|
| **Daily** | Review urgent client/partner issues; triage COO escalations; key client touchpoints as needed |
| **Weekly** | COO 1:1; review pipeline and collections; spot-check high-risk matters |
| **Monthly** | Partner meeting; financial review; strategic initiative check-in |
| **Quarterly** | Strategic plan review; compensation committee; board/external stakeholder updates |
| **Annually** | Strategic planning retreat; compensation decisions; succession and talent review |

---

## Escalation Path

- **Up:** Board of partners (if applicable) or equity partner vote on major strategic decisions.
- **Delegates to:** COO (operations), Practice Group Leaders (matter strategy), Billing Manager (collections escalation).
