# Document and Litigation Support Specialist

**Category:** Substantive Legal Support Personnel  
**Approval Level:** Medium—productions and filings require attorney sign-off

---

## Role Summary

The Document and Litigation Support Specialist manages electronic filing systems, coordinates e-discovery and document productions, and ensures strict compliance with court filing requirements. They bridge technology and legal process for litigation and transactional matters.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. E-Discovery and Document Production

- **E-discovery workflows:** Design and execute e-discovery workflows (collection, processing, review, production). Use Relativity, Everlaw, or similar platforms.
- **Data collection:** Coordinate with IT and custodians on data collection. Preserve chain of custody and metadata.
- **Processing:** Process collected data; manage load files, metadata, and searchability. Ensure defensibility.
- **Document review support:** Support attorney and paralegal document review; manage batches, tags, and coding.
- **Production:** Prepare productions per protocol; generate load files, logs, and documentation. Attorney approves before production.
- **Production logs:** Maintain production logs; track Bates numbers, document counts, and privilege designations.

### 2. Court Filing and E-Filing

- **E-filing systems:** Manage e-filing through PACER, state court systems, and other portals. Ensure compliance with local rules.
- **Filing compliance:** Verify document format, size limits, and procedural requirements before filing. Flag issues to attorney.
- **Service:** Coordinate service of filed documents per court rules. Maintain proof of service.
- **Deadline tracking:** Track filing deadlines; remind attorneys of upcoming filings. Escalate conflicts.
- **Court rules:** Maintain current knowledge of court rules and e-filing requirements. Update firm resources.

### 3. Document Management Systems

- **DMS administration:** Support document management system (Clio, NetDocuments, or similar). Ensure proper matter structure and permissions.
- **Document indexing:** Index documents with metadata for searchability and organization. Use consistent taxonomy.
- **Matter folders:** Create and maintain matter folder structure. Support matter opening and closing.
- **Version control:** Ensure version control and audit trails for critical documents.

### 4. Litigation Support Tools

- **Trial presentation:** Support trial presentation software (TrialDirector, etc.). Prepare exhibits and presentations.
- **Deposition support:** Support deposition video and transcript management. Prepare deposition designations and counter-designations.
- **Case management:** Support case management and timeline tools. Maintain case chronology and key document databases.
- **Vendor coordination:** Coordinate with e-discovery vendors, court reporters, and litigation support vendors.

### 5. Compliance and Best Practices

- **Compliance checks:** Verify productions and filings meet court requirements and protocols. Document compliance.
- **Privilege:** Support privilege review and log preparation. Ensure privilege designations are properly documented.
- **Retention:** Support document retention and litigation hold processes. Coordinate with Records Clerk and COO.
- **Training:** Train attorneys and staff on e-filing, e-discovery, and document management tools.
