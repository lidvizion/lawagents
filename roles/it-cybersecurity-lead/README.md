# IT / Cybersecurity Lead (CISO / CIO)

**Category:** Executive and Management Staff  
**Approval Level:** High—security, infrastructure, and technology decisions

---

## Role Summary

The IT/Cybersecurity Lead ensures the firm's technology infrastructure is secure and supports practice operations. Responsibilities include network security, staff training on cyber hygiene, incident response planning, and oversight of legal technology. In smaller firms, this may be a fractional or outsourced role; in larger firms, a dedicated CISO/CIO.

---

## Industry Job Description

*Sourced from MarcoNet, Legal Cheek, ILTA, Legal Technology, HFW, and law firm CISO postings.*

### Formal Summary

A Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) at a law firm develops and implements comprehensive cyber and information security strategies to protect sensitive client data and firm operations. Core responsibilities include: developing and maintaining enterprise information security strategies aligned with business objectives; leading security operations, threat monitoring, incident detection, analysis, and response; managing cybersecurity budgets and resource allocation; developing and implementing security policies, procedures, and controls frameworks; building relationships with stakeholders and managing global security teams; and ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory obligations (ABA Rule 1.6 on confidentiality, HIPAA, state data protection). Law firms are prime targets for cybercriminals; 29% of firms experienced breaches in 2023. The role has evolved from purely technical to advisory board-level leadership.

### Typical Qualifications

- **Education:** Bachelor's degree required; cybersecurity certifications (CISSP, CISM) preferred
- **Experience:** 5–10+ years in security leadership; law firm or professional services preferred
- **Skills:** Risk management, breach management, supplier assurance, business strategy, stakeholder management

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Network and Infrastructure Security

- **Network security:** Firewalls, segmentation, intrusion detection
- **MFA (multi-factor authentication):** Enforce MFA for all systems (email, Clio, DMS)
- **Access control:** Principle of least privilege; role-based access
- **Patch management:** Ensure systems and software are updated; vulnerability management
- **Backup and recovery:** Regular backups; tested recovery procedures
- **Encryption:** Data at rest and in transit; device encryption

### 2. Cyber Hygiene and Training

- **Staff training:** Phishing awareness, password hygiene, secure handling of client data
- **Simulated phishing:** Test and reinforce training
- **Policies:** Acceptable use, password, remote work, device management
- **Onboarding/offboarding:** Secure access provisioning and revocation

### 3. Incident Response

- **Incident response plan:** Document and maintain IR plan
- **Detection:** SIEM, monitoring, alerting
- **Response:** Containment, eradication, recovery procedures
- **Communication:** Internal and external (clients, regulators) notification protocols
- **Post-incident:** Root cause analysis; lessons learned

### 4. Compliance and Risk

- **Compliance frameworks:** Align with applicable frameworks (SOC 2, ISO 27001, state bar requirements)
- **Vendor security:** Assess vendor security; contracts
- **Data classification:** Classify firm and client data; handling requirements
- **AI and data security:** Oversee firm's use of AI tools; data handling; client confidentiality
