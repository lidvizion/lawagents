# Legal Intake Specialist

**Category:** Client Intake and Administrative Support  
**Approval Level:** Medium—case acceptance and engagement terms require attorney/partner approval

---

## Role Summary

The Legal Intake Specialist serves as the crucial first point of contact for potential clients. They gather essential case details, screen leads against the firm's criteria, manage the CRM database, and coordinate initial attorney consultations. They ensure the firm takes on profitable, appropriate matters while providing a positive first impression.

---

## Industry Job Description

*Sourced from Porter Firm, Indeed, Career.com, Provident Law, Jacobs & Jacobs, and law firm intake postings.*

### Formal Summary

Legal Intake Specialists manage the onboarding process for new and prospective clients. Core duties include: interviewing prospective clients by phone and email to understand their cases; conducting basic research to evaluate whether claims have merit; assessing whether cases align with the firm's practice areas; scheduling initial consultation appointments with attorneys; completing client intake paperwork and creating case files; entering client information into case management systems; performing follow-up communications via calls, emails, and texts; maintaining detailed records and updating case files as tasks are completed. Outstanding customer service skills and ability to demonstrate empathy are essential. Often the first human contact a potential client has with the firm.

### Typical Qualifications

- **Education:** High school diploma or GED; bachelor's degree preferred
- **Experience:** 1+ years performing intakes at a law firm
- **Skills:** Communication, interpersonal, organizational, detail-oriented; Microsoft Office; case management software; CRM; basic legal terminology

### Compensation Range

Typically $25–$45 per hour depending on location and experience. Benefits often include health insurance, 401(k) matching, dental, PTO.

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Lead Reception and Triage

- **First contact:** Answer intake calls and emails promptly. Provide professional, empathetic first impression.
- **Initial screening:** Gather basic information (name, contact, matter type, urgency). Determine if matter fits firm practice areas.
- **Triage:** Prioritize leads by urgency, matter type, and potential value. Route to appropriate attorney or next step.
- **Rejection handling:** Politely decline matters outside firm scope. Provide referrals when appropriate. Document rejection reason.
- **Follow-up:** Follow up on voicemails and web forms within firm SLA (e.g., 24 hours).

### 2. Intake Information Gathering

- **Intake forms:** Complete intake forms with case details, party names, key facts, and potential conflicts. Use firm intake system (e.g., Clio Grow).
- **Conflict pre-screening:** Collect party names and related entities for conflict check. Run preliminary conflict screen. Escalate potential conflicts to attorney.
- **Case criteria evaluation:** Evaluate matters against firm criteria (practice fit, profitability, capacity, conflict). Document evaluation.
- **Document collection:** Request and collect relevant documents (retainer agreements, prior counsel correspondence, key contracts). Ensure completeness for attorney review.
- **Consultation scheduling:** Schedule initial consultations with appropriate attorney. Confirm appointment and send confirmation/reminders.

### 3. CRM and Database Management

- **CRM data entry:** Enter all lead and intake data into CRM. Ensure accuracy and completeness.
- **Lead scoring:** Apply lead scoring criteria. Prioritize high-value, high-fit leads.
- **Pipeline tracking:** Maintain pipeline in CRM. Update status as leads progress or are declined.
- **Reporting:** Support Marketing and partners with intake metrics (volume, conversion, source, decline reasons).
- **Data hygiene:** Maintain clean CRM data; deduplicate; update contact information.

### 4. Engagement Coordination

- **Engagement letter preparation:** Prepare draft engagement letters per firm templates. Attorney customizes and approves.
- **Fee arrangement documentation:** Document fee arrangements (hourly, contingent, hybrid). Ensure clarity for client and firm.
- **Rejection/acceptance letters:** Draft rejection letters (with referrals when appropriate) and acceptance confirmations. Attorney approves before send.
- **Matter opening handoff:** Coordinate with Records Clerk and Billing Manager on matter opening. Ensure smooth transition from intake to active matter.

### 5. Process and Compliance

- **Intake protocols:** Follow firm intake protocols. Escalate exceptions to supervising attorney or COO.
- **Ethics compliance:** Ensure intake process complies with ethics rules (solicitation, conflicts, confidentiality).
- **Confidentiality:** Maintain strict confidentiality of all intake information. Do not discuss matters outside firm.
- **Quality:** Ensure consistent, professional intake experience. Seek feedback and improve process.
