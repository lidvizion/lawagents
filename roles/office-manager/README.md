# Office Manager

**Category:** Executive and Management Staff  
**Approval Level:** Low–Medium—routine operations; hiring and major purchases need approval

---

## Role Summary

The Office Manager handles day-to-day office functionality, non-attorney staff hiring, and general administration. In smaller firms within the $2M–$50M bracket, this role may overlap with or report to the COO. In larger firms, the COO handles strategy while the Office Manager focuses on execution.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Office Operations

- **Daily operations:** Ensure office opens and closes smoothly; manage mail, deliveries, and couriers.
- **Supplies:** Order and maintain office supplies, stationery, and consumables. Stay within budget.
- **Equipment:** Coordinate maintenance and repairs for copiers, printers, phones, and other equipment.
- **Vendor coordination:** Work with facilities, cleaning, and maintenance vendors. Escalate issues to COO.
- **Space management:** Manage office layout, seating, and space allocation. Support moves and reconfigurations.

### 2. Staff Scheduling and Coordination

- **Scheduling:** Coordinate staff schedules, coverage, and PTO. Ensure adequate coverage during absences.
- **Reception coverage:** Ensure front desk and phone coverage. Cross-train as needed.
- **Meeting coordination:** Support internal meeting scheduling, room booking, and setup.
- **Staff directory:** Maintain accurate staff directory, extension list, and org chart.

### 3. Non-Attorney Hiring and Onboarding

- **Recruiting:** Post positions, screen candidates, and coordinate interviews for non-attorney roles. Partner with COO on offers.
- **Onboarding:** Conduct new hire orientation for staff; coordinate with HR on paperwork and benefits.
- **Training:** Ensure new staff receive training on systems, procedures, and firm culture.
- **Offboarding:** Coordinate exit procedures; collect keys, badges, and equipment.

### 4. Administrative Support

- **Filing and records:** Support physical and electronic file organization as needed. Coordinate with Records Clerk.
- **Travel:** Assist with travel arrangements for staff as requested.
- **Events:** Support firm events, meetings, and celebrations. Coordinate catering and logistics.
- **Special projects:** Support COO and partners on administrative projects as assigned.

### 5. Facilities and Safety

- **Facilities:** Report maintenance issues; coordinate with landlord or facilities vendor.
- **Safety:** Support emergency procedures, evacuation plans, and safety compliance.
- **Access control:** Manage keys, badges, and access for staff and visitors.
