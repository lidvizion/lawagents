# Estate / Trust Officer (Non-Attorney)

**Category:** Substantive Legal Support / Legal Operations  
**Approval Level:** Medium—trust administration, filings require attorney oversight

---

## Role Summary

The Estate/Trust Officer oversees trust administration and probate-related filings. This role may be performed by a non-attorney (paralegal, trust administrator) or by an attorney depending on firm size. They handle trust accounting, court filings, and fiduciary reporting under attorney supervision.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Trust Administration

- **Trust accounting:** Maintain trust accounts; track assets, income, distributions, fees
- **Distributions:** Process distributions per trust terms; document beneficiary consent
- **Fiduciary reporting:** Prepare accountings for beneficiaries and courts
- **Trustee support:** Assist trustees with administrative duties; document decisions
- **Compliance:** Ensure trust administration complies with trust terms and state law

### 2. Probate Support

- **Court filings:** Prepare petitions, inventories, accountings for attorney review
- **Creditor claims:** Track notice periods; log claims; support objection process
- **Estate assets:** Inventory; track collection and liquidation
- **Distributions:** Support executor with distribution process; document

### 3. Accounting and Banking

- **Trust accounts:** Reconcile trust accounts; ensure IOLTA compliance
- **Accounting software:** Use QuickBooks, Xero, or estate-specific software
- **Banking integrations:** Pull trust account data for reconciliation
- **Disbursements:** Document authorization; maintain audit trail

### 4. Document Management

- **Legal document management:** Organize trust documents, court filings, accountings
- **Clio integration:** Matter management; document storage; task tracking
- **Retention:** Support document retention per firm policy
