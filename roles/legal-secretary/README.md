# Legal Secretary

**Category:** Client Intake and Administrative Support  
**Approval Level:** Low—routine administrative work

---

## Role Summary

The Legal Secretary focuses primarily on clerical and administrative duties: typing correspondence, answering phones, maintaining calendars, and managing files. They do not perform substantive legal analysis. They support attorneys and the broader legal team with efficient administrative execution.

---

## Industry Job Description

*Sourced from LG Law Firm, InterviewGuy, Glassdoor, Monster, BetterTeam, and law firm postings.*

### Formal Summary

Legal Secretaries provide administrative and clerical support to attorneys and legal professionals. Core duties include: document preparation (transcribing, formatting, editing, proofreading legal documents including letters, motions, appeals, petitions); calendar and scheduling management (maintaining attorney calendars, scheduling court depositions, hearings, conferences, meetings); file management (organizing, filing, maintaining client files in document management systems; ensuring court documents are filed before deadlines); communications (answering phones, managing emails, greeting clients, routing inquiries); administrative support (handling mail, entering billable time, preparing expense reports, e-filing with courts and state agencies); legal document creation (court documents, case citations, engagement letters, new matter reports); and research and verification (researching case information, authenticating legal details). Essential to maintaining office efficiency and case management.

### Typical Qualifications

- **Education:** High school diploma; Associate's or Bachelor's degree preferred
- **Experience:** 3+ years as legal secretary or paralegal
- **Skills:** Microsoft Office (Word, Outlook); 50+ WPM typing; legal terminology and procedures; confidentiality; time management; professional demeanor

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Correspondence and Typing

- **Typing and transcription:** Type correspondence, memos, and documents from dictation or drafts. Ensure accuracy and proper format.
- **Transcription:** Transcribe dictation, voicemails, or meeting notes as assigned. Maintain confidentiality.
- **Formatting:** Format documents per firm style. Use correct letterhead, margins, and layout.
- **Proofreading:** Proofread documents for typos, grammar, and format. Flag substantive questions to attorney.
- **Mail preparation:** Prepare outgoing mail, packages, and courier shipments. Track delivery when needed.

### 2. Phone and Communication

- **Phone coverage:** Answer attorney phones; take messages accurately. Triage and route calls appropriately.
- **Call logs:** Maintain call logs for matters when required. Document date, time, caller, and summary.
- **Client communication:** Relay messages to clients per attorney direction. Do not provide legal advice or substantive information.
- **Voicemail management:** Monitor and respond to voicemails. Ensure timely callback or escalation.

### 3. Calendar and Scheduling

- **Calendar maintenance:** Maintain attorney calendars; add appointments, deadlines, and court dates. Sync with firm calendar system.
- **Scheduling:** Schedule meetings, calls, and appointments per attorney direction. Coordinate with clients and internal staff.
- **Reminders:** Set reminders for deadlines and appointments. Alert attorney to conflicts or overload.
- **Travel support:** Assist with travel scheduling when requested. Provide itineraries and confirmations.

### 4. File Management

- **File organization:** Maintain organized matter files (electronic and physical). Use firm naming conventions.
- **Filing:** File documents in appropriate matter files. Ensure proper organization and retrieval.
- **Document retrieval:** Retrieve documents for attorney review. Support document production and copying as needed.
- **File maintenance:** Ensure files are current and complete. Coordinate with Records Clerk on retention.

### 5. Administrative Support

- **Copying and scanning:** Handle copying, scanning, and binding as needed. Ensure quality and completeness.
- **Expense documentation:** Assist with expense documentation and reimbursement as assigned.
- **Billing support:** Assist with time entry documentation and invoice preparation support as assigned.
- **Reception backup:** Provide reception backup when needed. Support front desk coverage.
