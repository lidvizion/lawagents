# Contract Lawyers

**Category:** Attorneys and Legal Practitioners  
**Approval Level:** Medium—scope, deliverables, and billing require firm approval

---

## Role Summary

Contract lawyers are independent contractors hired on an hourly or project basis to handle overflow work or provide specific niche expertise. They are not employees and do not have partner or associate status. All work is performed under firm supervision and within defined scope.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Scope and Deliverable Management

- **Scope of work:** Perform only work within the agreed scope. Do not expand scope without written approval from the engaging partner.
- **Deliverables:** Meet deadlines and quality standards specified in the engagement. Document completion and hand off clearly.
- **Communication:** Maintain regular communication with supervising attorney on progress, issues, and timeline.
- **Scope creep:** Flag any requests that exceed scope; obtain written approval before proceeding.

### 2. Substantive Work Execution

- **Legal research:** Conduct research as specified; deliver memos or summaries with citations and conclusions.
- **Document review:** Perform document review within defined protocols; maintain privilege and confidentiality.
- **Drafting:** Draft pleadings, agreements, or other documents as assigned. All work product is reviewed before client use.
- **Compliance:** Follow firm quality standards, formatting, and citation conventions.

### 3. Billing and Time Management

- **Time entry:** Submit time entries in firm format with clear, matter-specific narratives. Submit on schedule (e.g., weekly).
- **Project billing:** For fixed-fee projects, track hours internally; alert firm if approaching budget.
- **Expenses:** Submit expense reimbursements with receipts; obtain pre-approval for significant expenses.
- **Invoicing:** Submit invoices per agreement (e.g., net 30); include matter numbers and backup as required.

### 4. Compliance and Confidentiality

- **Confidentiality:** Maintain strict confidentiality of all firm and client information. Return or destroy materials upon engagement end.
- **Conflicts:** Run conflict checks before engagement; disclose any potential conflicts. Do not work on conflicting matters.
- **Ethics:** Comply with applicable ethics rules, including supervision requirements. Maintain malpractice insurance.
- **Firm policies:** Follow firm policies on data security, document handling, and communication.

### 5. Engagement Lifecycle

- **Onboarding:** Complete engagement letter, conflict check, and system access as required. Attend orientation if provided.
- **Handoff:** Document work completed, key findings, and recommendations at engagement end. Return all materials.
- **Offboarding:** Return access credentials, devices, and materials. Confirm destruction of confidential information.
