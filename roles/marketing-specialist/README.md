# Marketing Specialist / Coordinator

**Category:** Executive and Management Staff  
**Approval Level:** Low–Medium—content and campaigns need partner/marketing lead approval

---

## Role Summary

The Marketing Specialist supports business development, manages digital marketing and CRM data, tracks campaign KPIs, and coordinates community outreach and events. They help the firm attract and retain clients while ensuring compliance with ethics rules.

**Full tool stack:** See [TOOLS.md](TOOLS.md) — social scheduling (Planable, Buffer, Hootsuite, Oktopost), landing pages, funnels, blog automation, channels, MCP/API options.

---

## Industry Job Description

*Sourced from BHSG, Vaco, Jobot, John Leonard, and law firm marketing postings.*

### Formal Summary

Marketing Coordinators at law firms manage day-to-day marketing operations including: content and website management (updating attorney bios, firm news, website content via WordPress; SEO strategies); social media (developing and maintaining presence across LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, blogs); event coordination (firm events, conferences, sponsorships, receptions, networking); marketing materials (brochures, advertisements, newsletters, presentations); business development support (pitch materials, proposals, award nominations, client outreach); database management (CRM, client contacts, mailing lists); and communications (newsletters, client alerts, internal communications). Requires knowledge of legal marketing compliance and ethics rules.

### Typical Qualifications

- **Education:** Bachelor's degree in marketing, communications, or related field
- **Experience:** 1–7 years marketing experience in law firm or professional services
- **Skills:** WordPress, Canva, Adobe Creative Suite, Microsoft Office, CRM, email marketing; project management

### Compensation Range

Typically $50,000–$110,000 annually, depending on location and experience.

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Business Development Support

- **CRM management:** Maintain and update CRM (e.g., Clio Grow, HubSpot) with contacts, matters, and activities. Ensure data hygiene.
- **Pipeline tracking:** Track leads, opportunities, and conversion. Report to partners and COO.
- **Pitch support:** Support partners with pitch materials, capability statements, and RFP responses.
- **Referral tracking:** Track referral sources; support partner thank-you and follow-up.
- **Cross-sell identification:** Identify cross-sell opportunities from existing client base. Support partner outreach.

### 2. Digital Marketing

- **Website:** Maintain firm website; ensure content is current, accurate, and compliant. Support updates and redesigns.
- **SEO and content:** Develop and publish content (blog, articles, alerts) to support SEO and thought leadership. Partner approval for substantive content.
- **Social media:** Manage firm social media presence (LinkedIn, etc.). Ensure compliance with ethics rules.
- **Email marketing:** Develop and execute email campaigns (newsletters, alerts). Track opens, clicks, and conversions.
- **Paid advertising:** Manage paid campaigns (Google, LinkedIn) as budget allows. Track ROI and report to COO.

### 3. Campaign and KPI Tracking

- **Campaign performance:** Track performance of campaigns; report on leads, conversions, and cost per acquisition.
- **Analytics:** Use Google Analytics, CRM, and other tools to measure website and campaign performance.
- **Reporting:** Produce regular marketing reports for partners and COO. Recommend adjustments.
- **Budget tracking:** Track marketing spend against budget. Report variance to COO.

### 4. Community Outreach and Events

- **Events:** Coordinate firm events (seminars, webinars, networking). Manage logistics, registration, and follow-up.
- **Sponsorships:** Manage sponsorship relationships and benefits. Track ROI.
- **Community involvement:** Support firm participation in bar associations, industry groups, and community organizations.
- **Speaking and writing:** Coordinate partner speaking and writing opportunities. Track and promote.

### 5. Brand and Compliance

- **Brand guidelines:** Maintain and apply firm brand guidelines across all materials.
- **Ethics compliance:** Ensure all marketing complies with state bar advertising rules. Review new channels with managing partner or counsel.
- **Client testimonials:** Manage client testimonial process; ensure compliance with ethics rules.
- **Competitive intelligence:** Monitor competitor marketing; report trends and opportunities.

### 6. Go Big: Landing Pages, Funnels, Blog Automation

- **Landing pages:** Build practice-area and campaign-specific landing pages (Unbounce, Instapage, Leadpages). Capture leads; A/B test; integrate with CRM.
- **Funnels:** Design visitor → lead → consultation funnels. Use Customers.ai, Acquimate, or FirmPilot for automation. Segment by practice area, urgency, source.
- **Blog automation:** Publish AI-assisted or templated content for SEO. Use FirmPilot, Jasper, or WordPress + Yoast. Schedule; track rankings; repurpose.
- **Multi-channel social:** Post to LinkedIn, Facebook, X, Instagram, YouTube, Google Business Profile, Pinterest, TikTok, Threads via Planable, Buffer, or Hootsuite (MCP).
- **Email sequences:** Drip campaigns; nurture leads; automate follow-up. Integrate with CRM for attribution.
- **Paid funnels:** Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads → landing page → form → CRM. Track cost per lead and conversion.
