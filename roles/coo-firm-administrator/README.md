# Chief Operating Officer (COO) / Firm Administrator

**Category:** Executive and Management Staff  
**Approval Level:** High—budget, policy, and vendor decisions

---

## Role Summary

The COO manages the business side of the firm—human resources, finance, IT, marketing, and strategy—enabling partners to focus on practicing law and generating revenue. Essential for firms scaling in the $8M–$50M revenue bracket.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Financial Management

- **Budgeting:** Develop and manage firm-wide and department budgets. Track variance and report to Managing Partner.
- **Financial reporting:** Produce monthly P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, and KPI reports. Present to partnership.
- **Vendor management:** Negotiate contracts with key vendors (technology, insurance, facilities). Ensure cost-effectiveness.
- **Trust accounting:** Oversee trust account compliance; ensure proper handling of client funds per ethics rules.
- **Capital planning:** Support Managing Partner on major expenditures and financing decisions.

### 2. Human Resources

- **Recruiting:** Oversee non-attorney recruiting; partner with Office Manager on staff hiring. Support partner-led attorney recruiting.
- **Onboarding and offboarding:** Ensure consistent onboarding for new hires; manage offboarding and exit procedures.
- **Benefits and compensation:** Administer benefits programs; support compensation processes for staff.
- **Performance management:** Implement and maintain performance review processes for staff. Support partner-led attorney reviews.
- **Policy development:** Develop and maintain HR policies (handbook, PTO, remote work, etc.). Ensure legal compliance.
- **Employee relations:** Address employee concerns, conflicts, and disciplinary matters. Escalate to Managing Partner as needed.

### 3. Technology and Operations

- **Technology strategy:** Evaluate and implement practice management, billing, document management, and collaboration tools.
- **IT oversight:** Manage internal IT or vendor relationship; ensure security, backups, and support.
- **Process improvement:** Identify and implement process improvements across intake, billing, and matter management.
- **Vendor evaluation:** Compare and recommend technology and service vendors. Manage implementations.

### 4. Marketing and Business Development Support

- **Marketing oversight:** Work with Marketing Specialist on strategy, campaigns, and CRM. Ensure alignment with firm goals.
- **Budget and ROI:** Allocate marketing budget; track ROI on campaigns and initiatives.
- **Brand and compliance:** Ensure marketing complies with ethics rules and firm brand guidelines.

### 5. Facilities and Administration

- **Facilities:** Oversee office space, leases, maintenance, and security. Partner with Office Manager on day-to-day operations.
- **Compliance:** Ensure firm compliance with employment, insurance, and regulatory requirements.
- **Risk management:** Support malpractice and risk management; coordinate with insurance and legal counsel as needed.
