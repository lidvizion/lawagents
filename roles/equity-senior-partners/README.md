# Equity / Senior Partners

**Category:** Attorneys and Legal Practitioners  
**Approval Level:** High—client engagement, matter strategy, fee arrangements

---

## Role Summary

Equity partners are the owners of the firm. They handle high-profile clients, generate significant revenue, and receive a share of overall profits. They drive business development, set matter strategy, and lead teams.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Client and Matter Origination

- **Business development:** Actively cultivate new clients through referrals, speaking, writing, and networking. Track pipeline and conversion rates.
- **Matter intake:** Evaluate new matters for fit, profitability, and conflict. Approve engagement letters and fee arrangements.
- **Client retention:** Maintain strong relationships; anticipate needs and proactively offer value. Address billing and service issues before they escalate.
- **Cross-selling:** Identify opportunities to introduce other practice areas or partners to existing clients.

### 2. Matter Strategy and Oversight

- **Case strategy:** Set overall direction for complex matters; approve major tactical decisions (motion strategy, settlement parameters, trial approach).
- **Team leadership:** Assign work to associates and paralegals; balance workload and development opportunities.
- **Quality control:** Review substantive work product before client delivery. Ensure pleadings, briefs, and advice meet firm standards.
- **Budget and staffing:** Approve matter budgets, staffing plans, and use of contract lawyers or experts.

### 3. Revenue and Profitability

- **Billing oversight:** Review time entries and invoices; ensure accurate capture and appropriate write-ups/write-downs.
- **Fee arrangements:** Negotiate hourly, contingent, alternative fee, and hybrid arrangements. Document scope and expectations.
- **Collections:** Escalate aging accounts; approve write-offs and payment plans. Partner with Billing Manager on collection strategy.
- **Matter profitability:** Monitor realization rates, leverage, and matter economics. Take corrective action when matters underperform.

### 4. Team Development and Mentoring

- **Associate development:** Provide feedback, assign stretch work, and support partnership-track progression.
- **Paralegal and support:** Delegate appropriately; ensure support staff are trained and utilized effectively.
- **Succession:** Document client relationships and matter knowledge for continuity.

### 5. Firm Governance

- **Partnership participation:** Vote on firm governance, compensation, and strategic decisions. Serve on committees as assigned.
- **Compliance:** Ensure matters comply with ethics rules, conflicts, and firm policies. Escalate issues to Managing Partner or General Counsel.

## Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Routines

| Frequency | Activities |
|-----------|------------|
| **Daily** | Review matter updates; respond to client/associate questions; approve time entries and bills as needed |
| **Weekly** | Team meetings; matter strategy check-ins; pipeline review; billing review |
| **Monthly** | Client relationship check-ins; matter profitability review; associate feedback |
| **Quarterly** | Business development planning; cross-sell opportunities; practice area review |
| **Annually** | Compensation input; strategic planning participation; CLE and development |
