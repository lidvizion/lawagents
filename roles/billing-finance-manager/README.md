# Billing / Finance Manager

**Category:** Executive and Management Staff  
**Approval Level:** Medium—invoices and write-offs require partner/COO approval

---

## Role Summary

The Billing/Finance Manager oversees time entries, invoice preparation, collections, and financial records. They ensure accurate billing, timely invoicing, and effective collections while maintaining trust accounting compliance.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Time Entry Management

- **Time entry oversight:** Monitor time entry submission; follow up on missing or late entries. Ensure contemporaneous entry.
- **Narrative quality:** Review time entries for clarity and matter-specific detail. Request revisions when needed.
- **Write-up/write-down:** Support partners on write-up and write-down decisions. Document rationale.
- **Audits:** Conduct periodic time entry audits for accuracy and compliance. Report issues to COO and partners.
- **Training:** Train new attorneys and staff on time entry procedures and systems.

### 2. Invoice Preparation and Delivery

- **Invoice generation:** Generate invoices per firm schedule (e.g., monthly). Ensure accuracy of rates, amounts, and matter details.
- **Review process:** Route draft invoices to responsible attorneys for review. Incorporate revisions.
- **Delivery:** Send invoices to clients per engagement terms (email, portal, mail). Track delivery and acknowledgment.
- **Pro formas:** Prepare pro forma invoices for client approval when required.
- **Statements:** Generate and send account statements as needed.

### 3. Collections and Aging

- **Aging reports:** Produce and distribute aging reports to partners. Flag accounts requiring attention.
- **Collection follow-up:** Send payment reminders; coordinate with partners on collection calls and letters.
- **Payment plans:** Document and track payment arrangements. Ensure compliance with engagement terms.
- **Write-offs:** Prepare write-off recommendations; obtain partner approval. Document and process approved write-offs.
- **Escalation:** Escalate seriously delinquent accounts to Managing Partner or COO.

### 4. Financial Records and Reporting

- **Accounts receivable:** Maintain accurate AR records; reconcile with general ledger.
- **Matter profitability:** Produce matter profitability reports (realization, leverage, write-offs). Support partner decisions.
- **Trust accounting:** Ensure trust account transactions are properly recorded and reconciled. Support compliance.
- **Financial reporting:** Support COO with billing-related data for firm financial reports.

### 5. System and Process Management

- **Billing system:** Maintain billing system (e.g., Clio); ensure rates, matter codes, and templates are current.
- **Process improvement:** Identify and implement billing process improvements. Reduce cycle time and errors.
- **Client billing guidelines:** Track and apply client-specific billing guidelines. Flag non-compliance.
- **UTBMS and task codes:** Maintain matter and task code structure; support matter budgeting.
