# Legal Assistant

**Category:** Substantive Legal Support Personnel  
**Approval Level:** Low–Medium—routine procedural work; substantive drafts need review

---

## Role Summary

Legal Assistants handle a blend of procedural legal tasks and administrative duties. They focus on document management, scheduling, and basic drafting. While duties can overlap with paralegals in smaller firms, they typically focus less on deep legal research and more on procedural support and organization.

---

## Detailed Responsibilities

### 1. Document Management

- **Document formatting:** Format pleadings, letters, and other documents per firm style and court rules.
- **Document preparation:** Prepare documents for filing, service, or client delivery. Ensure proper formatting and assembly.
- **File organization:** Maintain organized matter files (electronic and physical). Use firm naming conventions.
- **Document retention:** Support document retention and destruction per firm policy. Coordinate with Records Clerk.
- **Transcription:** Transcribe dictation, meetings, or calls as assigned. Ensure accuracy and confidentiality.

### 2. Scheduling and Calendar Management

- **Calendar updates:** Maintain attorney calendars; add deadlines, court dates, meetings, and appointments.
- **Deadline tracking:** Track matter deadlines; remind attorneys of upcoming deadlines. Escalate conflicts.
- **Meeting coordination:** Schedule client meetings, depositions, and internal meetings. Coordinate with Receptionist as needed.
- **Travel:** Assist with travel arrangements when requested. Provide itineraries and confirmations.
- **Court scheduling:** Coordinate with courts and opposing counsel on scheduling when assigned.

### 3. Correspondence and Communication

- **Correspondence drafts:** Draft routine correspondence (cover letters, transmittal letters, acknowledgments) under attorney direction.
- **Client communications:** Assist with client communication logistics (sending documents, scheduling calls). Do not provide legal advice.
- **Phone coverage:** Answer attorney phones when needed; take messages and triage calls.
- **Email management:** Assist with email organization and follow-up as assigned. Maintain confidentiality.

### 4. Administrative Support

- **Filing and service:** Prepare documents for filing and service. Coordinate with Document & Litigation Support on e-filing.
- **Copying and scanning:** Handle copying, scanning, and document production as needed.
- **Billing support:** Assist with time entry backup, expense documentation, and invoice preparation support as assigned.
- **Intake support:** Assist Legal Intake Specialist with document collection and data entry when assigned.

### 5. Matter Support

- **Task tracking:** Track assigned tasks; report status to supervising attorney. Use firm task system.
- **Basic drafting:** Draft routine documents (cover letters, transmittal letters, simple notices) under attorney direction.
- **Document assembly:** Assemble documents for filings, productions, or client delivery. Ensure completeness.
- **Handoffs:** Document work completed and next steps when transitioning matters or tasks.
