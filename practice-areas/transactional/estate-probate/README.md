# Estate Planning / Probate

**Focus:** Wills, trusts, estate administration, probate procedure, tax planning for estates.

---

## What Estate/Probate Attorneys Do

- **Estate planning:** Wills, trusts (revocable, irrevocable), powers of attorney, healthcare directives
- **Probate:** Estate administration, court filings, creditor claims, distributions
- **Trust administration:** Trustee duties, distributions, accountings
- **Tax planning:** Estate tax, gift tax, generation-skipping; coordinate with CPAs
- **Estate litigation:** Will contests, trust disputes (overlap with litigation)
- **Charitable giving:** Charitable trusts, foundations

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Document and Template Management

- **Estate planning templates:** Wills, trusts, POA, healthcare directives—state-specific
- **Google Docs / Word:** Templates with variables; document assembly
- **Version control:** Track client-specific customizations; avoid errors
- **Execution:** Witness requirements; notarization; storage

### 2. Trust and Estate Accounting

- **Accounting software:** Estate accounting; track assets, distributions, fees
- **Trust accounts:** Banking APIs for trust account reconciliation
- **Fiduciary reporting:** Accountings for beneficiaries, courts
- **Tax coordination:** 706, 1041; work with accountants

### 3. Probate Workflow

- **Court filings:** Petitions, inventories, accountings
- **Creditor claims:** Notice, deadlines, objections
- **Distributions:** Per will/trust; document consent
- **Closing:** Final accountings, discharge

### 4. Banking and Trust Accounts

- **Trust account compliance:** IOLTA rules; separate client funds
- **Banking integrations:** Trust account data for reconciliation
- **Disbursements:** Document authorization; audit trail

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **State-specific** | Probate, trust law vary by state |
| **Tax deadlines** | 706 (9 months + extensions); 1041 |
| **IOLTA** | Trust account rules; compliance |
| **Fiduciary duty** | Trustee/executor obligations; document decisions |
| **Beneficiary communication** | Required notices; document delivery |
