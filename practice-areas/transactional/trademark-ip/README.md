# Trademark & IP (Transactional)

**Focus:** Trademark applications, prosecution, portfolio management, docketing, and IP lifecycle.

---

## What Trademark/IP Attorneys Do

- **Trademark prosecution:** Applications, office action responses, oppositions, renewals
- **Portfolio management:** Track multiple marks across jurisdictions
- **Docketing:** Track every deadline—office actions, use filings, Section 8/9 renewals
- **Due diligence:** IP audits for M&A, licensing
- **Licensing and assignments:** Draft and negotiate IP agreements

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Docketing Is Non-Negotiable

**Computerized docketing is essential.** The USPTO has sanctioned trademark practitioners for failing to use electronic docketing systems. Manual methods (calendars, spreadsheets) are insufficient.

- **Single trademark = dozen+ deadlines** over its lifecycle
- **Office action response:** Now **3 months** (shortened from 6 as of Dec 2022); $125 extension available if requested before deadline
- **Post-registration:** Section 8 (6-year) and Section 9 (10-year) maintenance filings
- **Multi-jurisdiction:** Madrid Protocol, foreign filings add complexity

### 2. Deadline Discipline

- **Enter every deadline** into docketing system immediately upon receipt
- **Standard procedures** for processing USPTO correspondence
- **Checks and balances**—secondary review of critical dates
- **Reminders** set well in advance (e.g., 30 days before office action due)

### 3. Office Action Response Workflow

- **3-month deadline** for most office actions (Madrid Protocol may differ)
- **Extension:** Request 3-month extension ($125) electronically **before** initial deadline
- **Petition to Revive:** Available if missed; must show unintentional delay + fee
- **Quality:** Thorough responses; distinguish cases; address all refusals

### 4. Portfolio Management

- **Central view** of all marks, statuses, and upcoming deadlines
- **Alert notifications** for critical dates
- **Renewal tracking** for Section 8 and Section 9
- **Expense tracking** for client billing and budgeting

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **USPTO deadline change (Dec 2022)** | Office action response shortened to 3 months | Missed deadline = abandonment |
| **Extension timing** | Must request extension before initial 3-month deadline | No extension after deadline |
| **Section 8/9** | 6- and 10-year maintenance | Grace periods exist but add cost |
| **Madrid Protocol** | Different deadlines may apply | Check jurisdiction-specific rules |
| **OED sanctions** | USPTO sanctions attorneys for poor docketing | Use electronic system |
| **Client communication** | Proactive updates on status and deadlines | Avoid surprises |

## Pain Points (Reddit, Industry)

- **Missed deadlines:** Abandonment, loss of client rights, recovery fees, OED discipline
- **Manual docketing:** Spreadsheets and calendars fail at scale; no secondary review
- **Office action volume:** 3-month deadline creates pressure; extension requests add admin
- **Portfolio sprawl:** Multiple marks, jurisdictions, and clients = complex tracking
- **Integration gaps:** Docketing systems may not integrate with practice management/billing
- **Cost:** Enterprise IP tools (Anaqua, etc.) expensive for small/mid firms
