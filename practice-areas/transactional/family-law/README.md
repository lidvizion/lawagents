# Family Law

**Focus:** Divorce, custody, support, adoption, domestic relations. Blend of transactional (agreements) and litigation (court proceedings).

---

## What Family Law Attorneys Do

- **Divorce:** Dissolution, property division, spousal support
- **Custody and visitation:** Parenting plans, custody agreements, modifications
- **Child support:** Calculations, enforcement, modifications
- **Adoption:** Domestic, international, stepparent
- **Prenuptial/postnuptial:** Agreements, enforcement
- **Mediation:** Facilitate settlement; some attorneys serve as mediators
- **Court appearances:** Hearings, trials, enforcement

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Client Intake and Sensitivity

- **Emotional support:** Clients often distressed; empathy and clear communication
- **Intake forms:** Comprehensive—assets, debts, children, income; Google Forms or Clio Grow
- **Conflict check:** Multiple parties in same matter; careful conflict screening
- **Retainer:** Often flat fee or retainer + hourly; document clearly

### 2. Document Assembly

- **Templates:** Petitions, responses, agreements, parenting plans
- **State-specific:** Family law is highly state-specific; local forms and procedures
- **Document assembly software:** HotDocs, Pathagoras, or firm templates
- **Court forms:** Many courts require specific forms; e-filing

### 3. Mediation and Settlement

- **Mediation platforms:** Virtual mediation tools; document sharing
- **Settlement agreements:** Draft, revise, finalize; court approval when required
- **Collaborative law:** Alternative process; team approach

### 4. Calendar and Deadlines

- **Court dates:** Hearings, trials, status conferences
- **Response deadlines:** Petitions, motions
- **Support modification:** Periodic review triggers

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **State-specific** | Custody, support, property division vary by state |
| **Best interest of child** | Custody standard; document factors |
| **Support guidelines** | State calculators; deviations require justification |
| **Confidentiality** | Highly sensitive; secure document handling |
| **Pro se opposing party** | Many family law matters have unrepresented parties |
