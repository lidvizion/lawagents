# Real Estate (Transactional)

**Focus:** Residential and commercial real estate closings, title, due diligence, and deal coordination.

---

## What Real Estate Attorneys Do

- **Residential closings:** Purchase/sale agreements, title review, closing disclosure, deed, mortgage documents
- **Commercial closings:** LOI, PSA, due diligence, title/survey, closing documents, post-closing
- **Title work:** Title search, title insurance, clearing title defects
- **Coordination:** Buyers, sellers, lenders, agents, title companies, surveyors
- **Escrow/deposits:** Manage earnest money, escrow instructions, disbursements

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Checklist Discipline

**Do not close without checklists.** Experienced attorneys stress this repeatedly. Checklists should cover:

- **Deadlines:** Due diligence, closing, title objection, financing contingency
- **Documents:** Contract exhibits, custom provisions, required forms (e.g., AS IS clauses)
- **Deposits:** Earnest money receipt, escrow instructions, release conditions
- **Title:** Title commitment review, survey review, lien clearance
- **Closing:** Closing disclosure, deed, mortgage, affidavits, transfer taxes

### 2. Deadline Management

- **Verify all key dates** immediately upon receiving a signed contract
- **"Time is of the essence"**—makes deadlines firm and non-negotiable
- **Track:** Due diligence deadline, financing contingency, closing date, title objection deadline
- **Calendar** every deadline in firm system; set reminders well in advance

### 3. Document Workflow

- **Pre-negotiated forms:** Check if contract includes exhibits that must be used
- **Custom provisions:** Ensure all negotiated terms appear in closing documents
- **Version control:** Use clear naming (e.g., `PSA_v3_2024-03-15_BuyerRev`) and track changes
- **Signature pages:** Automated generation from templates saves 60–80% of manual time (per Litera)

### 4. Multi-Party Coordination

- **Single source of truth:** Centralize deadlines, documents, and communications
- **Status updates:** Regular check-ins with all parties; document in writing
- **Missing items:** Track and follow up on missing documents; escalate early

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **Closing Disclosure timing** | TRID requires delivery 3 business days before closing |
| **Title insurance** | Buyer vs. seller policy; gap coverage; endorsements |
| **Survey** | Commercial deals often require current survey; exceptions matter |
| **Environmental** | Phase I/II for commercial; residential disclosures |
| **Escrow instructions** | Must match contract; errors can delay or kill closing |
| **Transfer taxes** | State and local; who pays per contract |
| **Certificate of occupancy** | Commercial tenants; residential new construction |

## Pain Points (Reddit, JD Supra, Industry)

- **Document chaos:** Endless email threads, missing documents, forgotten follow-ups
- **No standardized tooling:** Real estate attorneys lack central dashboards for deadlines, documents, deposits
- **Checklist adoption:** Many still close without formal checklists; risk of overlooking critical items
- **Multi-party communication:** Tracking who said what across buyers, sellers, lenders, agents
- **Deposit/escrow tracking:** Manual tracking of earnest money and release conditions
- **Scaling:** Volume closings strain manual processes; need automation for signature pages and checklists
