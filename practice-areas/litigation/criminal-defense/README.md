# Criminal Defense (Litigation)

**Focus:** Defense of individuals accused of crimes. Court-heavy, evidence-heavy, deadline-driven.

---

## What Criminal Defense Attorneys Do

- **Intake and retention:** Jail calls, court referrals, family referrals; urgency
- **Arraignment and bail:** First appearance, bail arguments, conditions
- **Discovery:** Prosecutor disclosure; defense investigation; evidence review
- **Motion practice:** Suppression, discovery, dismissal, speedy trial
- **Plea negotiation:** Negotiate with prosecutor; client counseling
- **Trial:** Jury selection, voir dire, opening, evidence, closing, sentencing
- **Appeals:** Post-conviction relief, appeals

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Intake and Urgency

- **Speed matters:** Clients often in custody; family anxious
- **Automate:** Answering service to pre-screen; scheduling links; template emails
- **Retention:** Fee agreement; often flat fee or payment plan
- **Conflict check:** Immediate; multiple defendants in same case

### 2. Court and Deadline Management

- **Court dates:** Arraignment, pretrial, motion hearings, trial
- **Speedy trial:** State and federal rules; statutory deadlines
- **Discovery deadlines:** Motion to compel; response deadlines
- **Filing deadlines:** Motions, responses, appeals

### 3. Evidence and Case Management

- **Discovery review:** Prosecutor's evidence; police reports, body cam, forensics
- **Defense investigation:** Witness interviews, experts, alibi
- **Evidence organization:** Volume can be massive; need structure
- **Document management:** Pleadings, motions, exhibits

### 4. Client Communication

- **Jail calls:** Document; client may have limited access
- **Family updates:** Manage expectations; explain process
- **Court appearances:** Client needs to know when/where

---

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **Speedy trial** | Constitutional right; statutory deadlines | Missed = dismissal or reversal |
| **Brady material** | Prosecutor must disclose exculpatory evidence | Motion to compel if withheld |
| **Conflict of interest** | Multiple defendants; prior representation | Must be cleared before engagement |
| **Client in custody** | Access, communication, urgency | Different workflow than civil |
| **Flat fee/common** | Less hourly tracking; matter profitability different | Track costs, time for internal metrics |

## Pain Points (Reddit, Law.com, Industry)

- **Solo workload:** Heavy caseloads; massive evidence piles; need tech to manage
- **Hiring vs. tech:** New solos debate hiring paralegal vs. investing in automation first
- **Client preference:** Referrals often want direct attorney contact; hard to delegate intake
- **Automation opportunity:** Intake forms, scheduling, template emails can free time for billable work
- **Tech as competitive edge:** Solos can move faster than big firms; adopt tools more easily
- **Staffing:** Law students as interns for admin; answering services for calls
