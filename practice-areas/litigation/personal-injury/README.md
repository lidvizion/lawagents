# Personal Injury (Litigation)

**Focus:** Plaintiff-side personal injury—auto accidents, medical malpractice, premises liability, wrongful death. Typically contingency fee.

---

## What PI Attorneys Do

- **Intake and screening:** Evaluate cases for merit, damages, liability; conflict check
- **Investigation:** Police reports, medical records, witnesses, experts
- **Demand and negotiation:** Demand letters, settlement negotiations, mediation
- **Litigation:** Complaints, discovery, depositions, trial
- **Settlement:** Liens, Medicare/Medicaid, subrogation, disbursement
- **Medical chronology:** Organize voluminous medical records; identify key dates, treatments, symptoms

---

## How to Operate

### 1. Intake and Case Screening

- **Criteria:** Liability, damages, collectability, conflict
- **Automate:** Electronic intake forms, automated tasks, scheduling links
- **Client expectations:** Contingency means no upfront fee; explain process clearly
- **Retention:** Engagement letter, medical auth, HIPAA, lien letters

### 2. Medical Records and Chronology

- **Volume:** PI cases often have thousands of pages of medical records
- **AI assist:** AI can automate medical chronology—extract dates, treatments, symptoms, medications
- **Organization:** Standardize into searchable format; multiple providers
- **Key events:** Identify symptom changes, medication events, treatment gaps

### 3. Case Management

- **Statute of limitations:** Automated SOL tracking is critical; missed SOL = malpractice
- **Demand/lien tracking:** Track demands, liens, subrogation, Medicare set-aside
- **Discovery management:** Interrogatories, RFPs, depositions; deadlines
- **Settlement calculator:** Track damages, liens, fees, costs for settlement analysis

### 4. Settlement and Disbursement

- **Lien resolution:** Medicare, Medicaid, ERISA, health insurance, child support
- **Disbursement:** Client share, fees, costs, liens; accounting
- **Closing:** Release, dismissal, file closure

---

## Things to Keep in Mind

| Item | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **Statute of limitations** | Varies by state and claim type | Missed SOL = malpractice |
| **Lien letters** | Send early; preserve rights | Reduce client recovery if not handled |
| **Medicare/Medicaid** | Mandatory reporting; set-aside for future care | Non-compliance = penalties |
| **Medical records** | Volume and cost; need organization | AI can reduce manual extraction |
| **Contingency billing** | No hourly tracking; matter profitability different | Track costs, time for internal metrics |

## Pain Points (Reddit, r/paralegal, Industry)

- **Filevine problems:** Bates stamping glitches, share link issues, poor UX for older attorneys, features "more work than worth"
- **Caret Legal:** Some firms abandoned migration mid-implementation; failed to deliver
- **Tool consolidation:** Firms want to consolidate MyCase, Clio, Dropbox, DocuSign into single system
- **Medical records volume:** Manual extraction from thousands of pages; AI can help
- **Intake automation:** Repetitive low-value tasks; need automation (forms, scheduling, screening)
- **SOL tracking:** Critical; missed SOL = malpractice; must be automated and reliable
