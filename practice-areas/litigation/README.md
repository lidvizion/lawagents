# Litigation Practice

**Focus:** Resolving disputes through negotiation, arbitration, or court proceedings.

---

## Overview

Litigators investigate claims, conduct discovery, prepare pleadings and motions, take depositions, and argue in court. Work is **adversarial** and **reactive**—responding to disputes, court deadlines, and opposing counsel. They frequently interact with courts, judges, juries, and opposing counsel.

## How Litigators Operate

### Workflow

- **Intake** → Investigation → Pleadings → Discovery → Motion practice → Trial/Settlement
- **Unpredictable pace**—court deadlines, opposing counsel, discovery disputes
- **Document-heavy**—pleadings, discovery, depositions, exhibits, trial prep
- **E-discovery**—critical for civil litigation; collection, review, production

### Key Skills

- Legal research and writing
- Strategic thinking under pressure
- Synthesizing facts and arguments
- Persuasive writing and oral advocacy

### Things to Keep in Mind

1. **Court deadlines are non-negotiable**—missed deadlines can mean default, dismissal, or sanctions.
2. **E-discovery and privilege**—AI speeds review but privilege coding remains subjective; human review needed.
3. **Statute of limitations**—automated SOL tracking is essential for PI and other practice areas.
4. **Client communication**—litigation is stressful; clients need updates and managed expectations.
5. **Billing models**—contingency (PI) vs. hourly; each requires different tracking and reporting.

## Pain Points (Reddit, Industry)

- **Tool fragmentation:** Firms managing Clio + Dropbox + DocuSign + spreadsheets
- **Filevine issues:** Glitches (bates stamping, share links), poor UX for older attorneys
- **Caret Legal:** Some firms abandoned migration mid-implementation
- **E-discovery costs:** Large datasets expensive; need cost-effective options (Goldfynch, Logikull)
- **AI and privilege:** GenAI less effective at privilege coding; subjective nature
- **Doc review attorney anxiety:** AI automation reducing traditional doc review jobs
- **Intake bottleneck:** Repetitive, low-value tasks; need automation
