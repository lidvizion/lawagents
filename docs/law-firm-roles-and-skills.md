# Law Firm Roles & Skills

**Target:** Small to mid-sized law firms ($2M–$50M annual revenue)  
**Purpose:** Define roles, responsibilities, and skills for agent-driven workflows. Designed to pair with tools/technologies (MCP, APIs) for autonomous agent execution with check-ins, approvals, and guardrails.

---

## 1. Attorneys and Legal Practitioners

### Managing Partner (CEO)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Daily firm operations, strategic initiatives, high-level client relationships. At $8M–$10M+ revenue, shifts from active practice to corporate CEO functions. |
| **Key Skills** | Strategic planning, P&L management, partnership governance, client relationship management, talent and succession planning, conflict resolution |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Strategic dashboards, partner compensation modeling, firm-wide KPIs, high-level client communications, board/partner meeting prep |
| **Approval Level** | High—strategic decisions, major client commitments, partnership changes |

### Equity / Senior Partners

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Firm ownership, high-profile clients, major revenue generation, profit sharing. |
| **Key Skills** | Practice-area expertise, business development, matter origination, team leadership, complex matter strategy |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Matter status summaries, client pitch materials, conflict checks, matter profitability analysis, delegation and workload balancing |
| **Approval Level** | High—client engagement, matter strategy, fee arrangements |

### Non-Equity / Junior Partners

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Major casework, team leadership, salary-based compensation (no profit share). |
| **Key Skills** | Substantive expertise, project management, associate mentoring, client service, case strategy |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Case research summaries, draft motions and briefs review, team task assignment, billing review, matter timelines |
| **Approval Level** | Medium–High—substantive work product, client communications |

### Associates

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Daily substantive case tasks, representation under partner supervision, partnership track. |
| **Key Skills** | Legal research, drafting, discovery, court preparation, time management, matter organization |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Research memos, draft pleadings, document review coordination, calendar management, conflict checks, cite checking |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—work product requires partner review before client delivery |

### Of Counsel

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Ongoing affiliation without partner or associate status; niche expertise or part-time engagement. |
| **Key Skills** | Specialized practice area, advisory work, relationship maintenance |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Advisory memos, matter-specific research, conflict screening, matter handoff documentation |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—advisory output, engagement terms |

### Contract Lawyers

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Independent contractors; overflow work or niche expertise on hourly/project basis. |
| **Key Skills** | Niche expertise, project-based delivery, compliance with firm standards |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Scope-of-work documents, time entry templates, matter handoff checklists, quality review criteria |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—scope, deliverables, and billing require firm approval |

## 2. Executive and Management Staff

### Chief Operating Officer (COO) / Firm Administrator

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Business operations: HR, finance, IT, marketing, strategy. Enables partners to focus on practice and revenue. |
| **Key Skills** | Operations management, budgeting, HR policies, vendor management, technology strategy, process improvement |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Financial reports, vendor comparisons, policy drafts, compliance tracking, staff utilization, technology evaluations |
| **Approval Level** | High—budget, policy, and vendor decisions |

### Office Manager

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Day-to-day office operations, non-attorney staff hiring, general administration (smaller firms in bracket). |
| **Key Skills** | Office administration, staff scheduling, facilities, supplies, vendor coordination |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Schedule coordination, supply ordering, facility maintenance tracking, staff directory updates |
| **Approval Level** | Low–Medium—routine operations; hiring and major purchases need approval |

### Billing / Finance Manager

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Time entries, invoice preparation, collections, financial records. |
| **Key Skills** | Billing systems, trust accounting, collections, financial reporting, matter profitability |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Invoice generation, aging reports, write-off analysis, matter profitability reports, time entry audits |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—invoices and write-offs require partner/COO approval |

### Marketing Specialist / Coordinator

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Business development, digital marketing, CRM, campaign KPIs, community outreach and events. |
| **Key Skills** | Marketing strategy, CRM management, content creation, event coordination, analytics |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Campaign performance reports, CRM data hygiene, content drafts, event logistics, lead tracking |
| **Approval Level** | Low–Medium—content and campaigns need partner/marketing lead approval |

## 3. Substantive Legal Support Personnel

### Paralegal

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Substantive legal work under attorney supervision: research, witness interviews, drafting, trial prep. Often certified; may specialize (e.g., litigation, liability). |
| **Key Skills** | Legal research, document drafting, discovery management, trial preparation, case organization |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Research summaries, document assembly, deposition prep, exhibit lists, discovery responses, filing checklists |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—all substantive output requires attorney review |

### Legal Assistant

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Procedural legal tasks and administrative duties; document management, scheduling, basic drafting. Less deep research than paralegals. |
| **Key Skills** | Document management, scheduling, correspondence, file organization, basic legal formatting |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Document formatting, calendar updates, correspondence drafts, file organization, deadline tracking |
| **Approval Level** | Low–Medium—routine procedural work; substantive drafts need review |

### Document and Litigation Support Specialist

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | E-filing systems, e-discovery, document productions, court filing compliance. |
| **Key Skills** | E-discovery platforms, litigation support tools, court rules, document management systems |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | E-discovery workflows, production logs, filing compliance checks, document indexing, metadata management |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—productions and filings require attorney sign-off |

---

## 4. Client Intake and Administrative Support

### Legal Intake Specialist

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | First point of contact for potential clients; case details, lead screening, CRM, initial attorney consultations. Ensures firm takes on profitable, appropriate matters. |
| **Key Skills** | Intake protocols, conflict screening, CRM management, empathy and communication, case criteria evaluation |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Intake form completion, conflict pre-screening, CRM data entry, consultation scheduling, lead scoring, rejection/acceptance letters |
| **Approval Level** | Medium—case acceptance and engagement terms require attorney/partner approval |

### Legal Secretary

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Clerical and administrative: correspondence, phones, calendars, files. No substantive legal analysis. |
| **Key Skills** | Typing, transcription, calendar management, file management, professional communication |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Correspondence drafts, calendar updates, call logs, file organization, transcription |
| **Approval Level** | Low—routine administrative work |

### Records Clerk / Case Opener (Data Entry)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | File organization, maintenance, tracking; review of initial intake documentation for completeness. |
| **Key Skills** | File management, data entry, document organization, naming conventions |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | File creation, document indexing, intake completeness checks, matter number assignment |
| **Approval Level** | Low—data entry and file setup |

### Receptionist / Front Desk Assistant

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Responsibilities** | Front-of-house operations, client greetings, basic clerical support. |
| **Key Skills** | Client service, phone etiquette, scheduling, visitor management |
| **Agent-Ready Tasks** | Appointment scheduling, visitor logs, call routing, basic intake triage |
| **Approval Level** | Low—routine front-desk operations |

## 5. Agent Workflow Summary

| Role Category | Typical Approval Level | Primary Agent Use Cases |
|---------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Attorneys (Partners) | High | Strategy, client relations, matter oversight |
| Attorneys (Associates) | Medium | Research, drafting, matter support |
| Executive/Management | High–Medium | Reporting, policy, vendor decisions |
| Substantive Support | Medium | Research, documents, discovery |
| Intake/Admin | Low–Medium | Intake, scheduling, data entry |
